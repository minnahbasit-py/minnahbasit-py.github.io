about me

If I had to describe my childhood, I would say it was a journey of a nomad, moving countries from Kentucky to California to Pakistan to the UAE and finally back here to California where I attend the University of Southern California. Having changed schools 13 times due to familial circumstances has shaped my perspective to have a deep appreciation for different cultures while also maintaining mine.

My interest in technology and product innovation started when I competed in hackathons out of pure boredom in high school. At my first hackathon, the ElevateHacks Hackathon, my team and I created a comprehensive website addressing mental health, academics, and career goals.

After gaining more experience in building websites I got to compete in the BusiHacks hackathon. Here I not only competed but led my team in developing a web interface for financial education. The project involved creating a platform that promotes financial literacy with a built-in mock-trading simulation feature. I took charge of the front-end web development and designed a user-friendly display.

After competing in many hackathons I wanted to branch my skillset to app development. As a Kode With Klossy App Developer, I created the mobile app "Greenhouse," to empower uses with knowledge about sustainability within the products they use and alternatives to these.

Now at USC, I am working on a startup idea to connect USC students for ride-sharing during academic breaks to the airport. I am working on building the app after having done the market research and user discovery for this idea.
